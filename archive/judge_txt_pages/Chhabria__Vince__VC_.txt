***IMPORTANT NOTICE REGARDING CASES BEFORE JUDGE CHHABRIA***
Civil Case Management Calendar
:
Case management conferences will take place via Zoom on
Fridays, at 10:00 a.m.
Case management conferences can
be viewed by the public through the Zoom link on Judge Chhabria’s webpage.
In-person Civil Law and Motion Calendar
(see below for available dates for Civil Law and Motion Hearings):
All civil motions, except for
motions for preliminary or final approval of class action settlements or
motions for default judgment, must be noticed for in-person hearing on Thursdays
at 10:00 a.m.
Absent extraordinary
circumstances, the Court will not hold hybrid hearings (with some counsel
appearing in person and some remotely). Parties may file a joint stipulation or
an administrative motion requesting that the Court convert an in-person hearing
to Zoom and set it for the 2:00PM calendar. Such requests should be filed by
the Monday before the hearing. The Court may deny the request if it believes an
in-person hearing would be more beneficial.
The Court may, on its own
accord, issue a clerk’s notice converting and rescheduling an in-person hearing
to the 2:00 p.m. Zoom calendar. Counsel is responsible for knowing whether the
hearing is in person or over Zoom and should check the docket in preparation
for the hearing.
Members of the public can
listen to audio of all civil in-person hearings through the Zoom link on Judge
Chhabria’s webpage.
Zoom Civil Law and Motion
Civil motions being heard over Zoom take place
Thursdays at 2:00 p.m. Motions for preliminary or final approval of class
action settlements and motions for default judgment must be noticed for this Zoom
calendar. As discussed in the previous section, some other motions that are
normally heard in person may be moved to the Zoom calendar.
Members of the public can view these hearings
through the Zoom link on Judge Chhabria’s webpage.
Criminal Calendar
:
In-person calendar, Wednesdays, at 1:00 p.m.
Regardless of whether a defendant is in custody, all of the following proceedings will take place in person, with the defendant present:
·
Initial appearances
·
Substantive motion hearings
·
Change of pleas
·
Sentencings
·
Any hearing or status conference relating to an alleged supervised release violation
·
Any other hearing or status conference where either the defendant or government requests that the matter be held in person.
Typically, Zoom audio access to
in-person criminal proceedings will not be provided; interested members of the
public can observe proceedings in the courtroom. In special circumstances and
with advance agreement by the government and the defendant to waive any Rule 53
objections, the Court will consider providing remote audio access to an
in-person criminal proceeding.
Zoom calendar, Wednesday
at 11:00 a.m.
For any proceedings other than
the ones described above (for example, subsequent status conferences,
scheduling conferences, or trial settings), the attorneys and the defendant
will appear by Zoom, so long as both sides consent and agree on the record to
waive any Rule 53 objections.
