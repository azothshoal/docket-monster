Judge Davila's Law and Motion are scheduled on available calendar dates as follows:
I. CIVIL MOTIONS
Civil Motions are scheduled on
THURSDAYS at 9:00 AM –
by
RESERVATION ONLY
.
To reserve a hearing date please contact Judge Davila’s Courtroom Deputy, Cheré Robinson via email at
ejdcrd@cand.uscourts.gov
.
Once your matter is reserved for hearing, your motion should be noticed for the reserved date and in accordance with the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure and the Local Civil Rules.
If the respective party fails to file the motion within 2-weeks after reserving a hearing date or in accordance with the Federal and Local Rules, the reserved calendar date will be vacated without further notice to the parties.
PLEASE NOTE:
Please also refer to Judge Davila’s
Standing Order re Civil Cases
.
Judge Davila does not issue tentative rulings on upcoming matters.
Any matters that are taken under submission by the Court are submitted until further Order of the Court (L.R. 7-1(b)).
II. CASE MANAGEMENT AND TRIAL SETTING CONFERENCES
Civil Case Management Conferences and Trial Setting Conferences are scheduled for
THURSDAYS at 10:00 AM and 11:00 AM,
respectively.
III. CRIMINAL MATTERS
Criminal matters are set on
Mondays at 1:30 PM.
PLEASE NOTE:
1.  Draft (unsigned) versions of the proposed plea agreement should be submitted to the Courtroom Deputy not less than three court days prior to the scheduled hearing.
2.  Judge Davila does not use the Court’s “Guilty Plea Application and Order” form for individuals wishing to plea open (
e.g.
, without a plea agreement).  The court will hold
voir dire
of the defendant re: change of plea in the normal course.
3.  Deadline to file Stipulation/Proposed Order to continue hearing is three court days prior to scheduled hearing.
IV. TRIAL SCHEDULING
Jury Selection: Fridays or Tuesdays at 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM
Jury Trial:  Tuesdays, Wednesdays and Fridays from 9:00 AM to 4:30 PM
NOTE:
The Court may also set additional trial days/times on Mondays and Thursday at its discretion.
OTHER SCHEDULING ITEMS AND FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS:
V. REMOTE APPEARANCES
All hearings are set
in-person
.
The Court will not grant, absent extraordinary circumstances, a request to appear remotely for parties or counsel who reside or have an office located within the boundaries of the Northern District of California (counties of Alameda, Contra Costa, Del Norte, Humboldt, Lake, Marin, Mendocino, Monterey, Napa, San Benito, San Francisco, San Mateo, Santa Clara, Santa Cruz, and Sonoma).
In addition
, the Court will not grant a request by the moving party on a motion.
T
he Court will consider requests to appear remotely on a case-by-case and hearing-by-hearing basis.
Parties wishing to appear remotely are directed to file and serve an Administrative Request and Proposed Order no less than one week prior to the scheduled hearing date.
After consideration of the request by the Court, an order will be issued approving or denying the request.
The parties are directed to contact the Courtroom Deputy for instructions on appearance for any civil matters that have been set by the Court to be held telephonically.  The Courtroom Deputy will provide a dial-in number and information for individuals permitted to appear telephonically.
Telephonic
participants are requested to have their devices remain muted unless they are addressing the Court.
Public proceedings held remotely are available to be viewed by Members of the Press and the Public in the Courtroom.
VI.
PENDING STIPULATIONS/ADMINISTRATIVE MOTIONS REGARDING CONTINUANCE/MISCELLANEOUS
REQUESTS; LETTERS/LETTER BRIEFS
Once a stipulation and proposed order OR an administrative m
otion has
been filed with the Court, it is submitted until further order of the Court.
All Court Orders are sent by electronic notice
to the e-mail addresses on record.
Pro-se
parties will receive notice by postal mail to the address on record if such party does not have ECF filing privileges and receive ECF notification.
The Court
does not respond to letters or letter briefs seeking legal relief from the
Court, unless leave to make such a request has been specifically granted.
All requests requiring the Court to take
action shall be made by either a stipulation and proposed order or by
appropriate motion made pursuant to the Local Rules.
VII.
CHAMBERS COPIES
The
parties are REQUIRED to provide chamber copies of
only
the pleadings and
dispositive motion briefing to the Clerk’s Office the business
day following the day of filing.
These
printed copies shall be marked “Chambers Copy” and shall be submitted in an envelope
clearly marked with the Judge’s name, case number, and “Chambers Copy-Do Not
File.”
