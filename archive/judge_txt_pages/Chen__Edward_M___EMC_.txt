***IMPORTANT NOTICE REGARDING CASES BEFORE JUDGE CHEN***
Effective June 1, 2024, all civil law and  motion hearings and civil pretrial conferences will be held in-person in Courtroom 5, 17th Floor.  All parties are required to arrive 30 minutes prior to the scheduled hearing time to check-in with the Courtroom Deputy.  If counsel wishes to appear by Zoom, such request (and reason therefor) must be filed on the docket and shall be made no later than 14 days in advance of the hearing.
All criminal hearings and conferences will remain scheduled by videoconference on Wednesdays, unless an 
in-person hearing is requested.  In-Person hearings on criminal matters are now available to be set on Thursdays mornings from 9AM - 12:00PM noon.  All live in-court appearances will be held in Courtroom #5, 17th Floor.
Initial Case Management Conferences, Further Case Management Conferences, and Status Conferences in all civil matters are now regularly scheduled on Tuesdays at 1:30PM and 2:30PM, respectively, effective July 1, 2021. These hearings will be conducted via Zoom.   Standing Order revised.
CHAMBER COPIES:
Hard chamber copies no longer required. Exceptions to this are pretrial conferences, summary judgments, claims constructions.  Parties are to review revised Civil Standing Order - General for further details.
CIVIL LAW AND MOTION HEARINGS:
The Court will generally
limit the number of motions on any civil law-and-motion date to 6 matters;
counsel should not notice a motion on a date that already has 6 cases with a
motion(s) pending.
If 6 matters are already
pending but there is good cause to add another matter to the calendar, a party
may file an administrative request for relief at the time it files its
substantive motion, asking for its matter to be added to the otherwise full
calendar.  There may be good cause where, e.g., a party is moving for a
temporary restraining order or preliminary injunction; a party is filing a
motion for summary judgment in accordance with the schedule laid out in the
pretrial order; or a party's motion needs resolution based on other deadlines
set by the Court.
